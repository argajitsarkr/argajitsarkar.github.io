Argajit Sarkar

I am a first-year PhD candidate in the Department of Molecular Biology and Bioinformatics at Tripura University, under the guidance of Dr. Surajit Bhattacharjee, Associate Professor and Head of Department.

My research integrates microbiology with machine learning to predict antimicrobial resistance outbreaks. Transitioning from wet-lab techniques to computational methods, I'm motivated to develop models that analyze pathogen genomics and resistance patterns. My work combines Machine learning prediction with targeted laboratory validation.

Prior to this, I had worked at the laboratory of Molecular biology and bioinformatics, where we explored the Combinatorial Antimicrobial and Anti-Virulence Potential of Quercetin (Plant based flavanoid) with Antibiotics Against Vibrio cholerae (Gram-negative bac causing outbreaks in India).

I received my B.Sc in Human physiology at Holy cross college, Tripura, India.

Biofilm-mediated bioremediation of xenobiotics and heavy metals: a comprehensive review of microbial ecology, molecular mechanisms, and emerging biotechnological applications
Argajit Sarkar, Surajit Bhattacharjee,
3 Biotech, 2025
paper

This comprehensive review explores the potential of bacterial biofilms in bioremediation, focusing on their ability to degrade and transform a wide array of pollutants, including heavy metals, persistent organic pollutants (POPs), oil spills, pesticides, and emerging contaminants, such as pharmaceuticals and microplastics.
TGF-beta plays dual roles in immunity and pathogenesis in leishmaniasis
Susmita Barik, Sanghamitra Goswami, Prakash Kumar Nanda, Argajit Sarkar, Bhaskar Saha, Arup Sarkar, Surajit Bhattacharjee,
Cytokine, 2025
paper

Transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-β), displaying a dual role in immunosuppression and pathogenesis, has emerged as a key regulator of anti-leishmanial immune responses. In Leishmania infections, TGF-β drives immune deviation by enhancing regulatory T-cell (T-reg) differentiation and inhibiting macrophage activation, suppressing critical antiparasitic responses.
